# Write-up

## Why these questions?

The tree is designed to capture three phases:

1. Awareness (current state identification)
2. Action (decision and execution)
3. Reflection (learning and feedback)

Each question narrows the user's state without leading them.

---

## Branching Design

* Questions use fixed options (3–4) to standardize paths
* Decision nodes model internal reasoning (fear, confusion, intention)
* Bridge node ensures transition into reflection phase

---

## Trade-offs

* Limited options vs depth: kept concise for clarity
* Generic vs personalized: designed for broad applicability

---

## Psychological Basis

* Habit loop (cue → action → outcome)
* Cognitive overload leads to avoidance
* Reflection improves future decisions

---

## Improvements

* Add personalization based on past sessions
* Introduce probabilistic branching
* Track long-term behavior patterns
